# GM Workspace

## Mission

GM Workspace is the executive decision-support platform inside Portal Pulse Pro. It helps General Managers, Player Personnel Directors, Recruiting Directors, Athletic Directors, and executive staff evaluate long-term roster construction, transfer strategy, scholarship allocation, recruiting investment, and organizational risk.

GM Workspace is not a recruiting CRM. It is a strategic intelligence layer that organizes verified information, analysis, projections, and simulations into an executive operating view.

## Target Users

- General Managers
- Player Personnel Directors
- Recruiting Directors
- Athletic Directors
- Executive staff
- Future enterprise partners

## Executive Philosophy

The workspace answers leadership questions:

- What are the highest organizational risks?
- Where should scholarships be allocated?
- Which transfer targets maximize roster value?
- Which position groups require investment?
- How does today's roster compare to next season?
- Which opportunities should leadership discuss today?

Every recommendation must be explainable, confidence-scored, and clearly classified as verified placeholder, analysis, projection, simulation, or mock.

## Core Modules

- **Executive Brief:** top priorities, critical risks, emerging opportunities, market summary, recruiting status, roster forecast, historical context, and AI executive summary.
- **Roster Health Score:** executive metric combining depth, experience, class balance, scholarship pressure, transfer dependency, roster chemistry, and leadership continuity.
- **Scholarship IQ:** long-term scholarship allocation, projected openings, position overloads, future shortages, and recommended actions.
- **Transfer Portfolio:** strategic transfer board organized by high priority, development targets, immediate contributors, future fits, monitoring, and committed-elsewhere placeholders.
- **Recruiting Pipeline:** regional focus, position priorities, future class balance, relationship placeholders, historical trend, and Cartographer context.
- **Organizational Planning:** three-year outlook, five-year scholarship outlook, recruiting allocation, transfer allocation, risk management, scenario planning, and Labs integration.
- **AI Executive Council:** Athena, Atlas, Mercury, Apollo, Oracle, Librarian, and Cartographer provide specialized mock recommendations that remain subject to Intelligence Core review.
- **Investment Board:** roster assets, position investments, future opportunities, transfer investments, development pipeline, and retention priorities.
- **Reports:** executive daily report, weekly personnel report, roster health report, scholarship outlook, transfer portfolio, recruiting outlook, program health summary, and board presentation placeholders.

## Relationship to Coach Workspace

Coach Workspace should eventually focus on daily staff execution: practice priorities, position room management, recruiting board workflows, visit planning, and staff collaboration.

GM Workspace focuses on executive decisions: multi-year roster strategy, resource allocation, portfolio management, risk, reporting, and program-level planning.

## Relationship to Atlas

GM Workspace consumes Atlas concepts rather than duplicating them:

- Roster DNA
- Team Genome
- Roster Chemistry
- Scholarship IQ
- Transfer Fit
- Opportunity Engine
- Strategy Engine
- Simulations
- Forecasts

Atlas remains the roster intelligence analyst. GM Workspace turns those signals into executive decisions and reporting workflows.

## Relationship to Labs

Portal Pulse Labs is the safe environment for simulations. GM Workspace may recommend scenarios to run in Labs, but simulated outputs must remain clearly separated from verified intelligence.

## Trust Rules

- Mock data must include `isMock: true`.
- Simulations are never facts.
- Projections are never commitments.
- NIL values and scholarship counts must not be presented as real unless sourced and verified in a future phase.
- AI agents may propose executive recommendations, but the Intelligence Core determines what can surface.
- Every future real recommendation must include evidence, confidence, source counts, timestamps, and contradiction checks.
