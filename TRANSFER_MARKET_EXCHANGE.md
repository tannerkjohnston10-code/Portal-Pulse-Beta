# Transfer Market Exchange

## Market Philosophy

The Transfer Market Exchange, or TMX, is Portal Pulse's transfer movement intelligence surface. It uses a markets-style dashboard language to explain attention, activity, momentum, demand, competition, confidence, relationship strength, and evidence volume.

This is an analytical metaphor only. TMX must never imply that players are financial assets, commodities, or tradable securities.

## Metrics

TMX metrics are explainable placeholders in this phase:

- Portal Activity Index
- Player Momentum
- School Momentum
- Conference Momentum
- Transfer Velocity
- Recruiting Heat
- Position Scarcity
- Market Confidence
- Interest Volume
- Signal Strength
- Prediction Velocity
- Source Activity

Every metric must include confidence, source count, evidence count, last updated timestamp, and an explanation. Mock records must include `isMock: true`.

## Momentum

The momentum engine prepares reusable calculations for momentum, acceleration, volatility, trend, confidence, consistency, signal growth, relationship growth, and interest growth.

Future versions should compute momentum from verified claims, Sentinel intake records, Knowledge Graph relationships, Oracle prediction movement, Apollo historical context, and user watchlists.

## Market Dashboard

The `/market` dashboard includes market summary, today's movers, trending players, trending teams, conference activity, position markets, prediction movers, interest leaders, source activity, market timeline, heat index, watchlist activity, and market commentary.

The dashboard consumes `PortalPulsePlatform.market` and should not duplicate market logic locally.

## Intelligence Core Relationship

Mercury may generate market commentary and proposed findings, but Mercury never publishes directly. The Intelligence Core must verify evidence, apply confidence scoring, check conflicts, and decide what can surface in UI, APIs, alerts, or briefings.

TMX must separate verified facts, source-backed claims, market signals, intelligence, predictions, and recommendations.

## Apollo Relationship

TMX integrates with Apollo by linking market timeline events to historical replay context. Future versions should support current market versus prior snapshot, this week versus last week, market heat replay, prediction velocity history, position scarcity history, and conference movement history.

Apollo never rewrites old market snapshots. It preserves what Portal Pulse knew at the time.
