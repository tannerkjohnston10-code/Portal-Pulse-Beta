# Volume III — The Company

## Business Unit Strategy

Portal Pulse can grow through three business units: Portal Pulse Intelligence, Portal Pulse Pro, and Portal Pulse Data.

Each unit should share the same trust architecture, platform services, and intelligence standards.

## Portal Pulse Intelligence

For fans, media, analysts, and content creators.

Primary products include Command Center, TMX, rankings, maps, Athena, player/team intelligence, and historical replay.

## Portal Pulse Pro

For coaches, recruiting staffs, personnel departments, and athletic directors.

Primary products include Atlas, Coach Workspace, GM Workspace, roster simulator, recruiting board, scholarship planning, Transfer Fit, and team strategy tools.

## Portal Pulse Data

For media companies, developers, universities, partners, and research organizations.

Primary products include APIs, webhooks, data exports, white-label widgets, historical datasets, and enterprise reporting.

## Target Customers

Target customers include fans, super fans, media companies, podcasts, coaches, recruiting departments, athletic directors, NIL collectives, developers, enterprise partners, and research organizations.

## Revenue Model

Planned revenue paths include free access, fan subscriptions, premium fan tiers, media/research tiers, team subscriptions, athletic department enterprise tiers, API tiers, data licensing, white-label widgets, mobile premium, and future marketplace products.

## Go-To-Market Strategy

The likely first wedge is a premium public intelligence product around the transfer portal. From there, Portal Pulse can expand into media workflows, premium fan subscriptions, team products, APIs, and enterprise dashboards.

## Media Strategy

Media strategy should prioritize fast research surfaces, embeddable intelligence widgets, source-aware summaries, explainable rankings, and historical context.

## Enterprise Strategy

Enterprise strategy should prioritize trust, privacy, auditability, role-based access, saved views, and integration with existing department workflows.

## API Strategy

Portal Pulse Data should expose versioned, source-aware, confidence-aware, timestamped, paginated, and safe API products.

## Mobile Strategy

Mobile should focus on alerts, watchlists, briefings, saved views, player/team tracking, and quick intelligence summaries.

## Partnership Strategy

Potential partners include media companies, data platforms, universities, athletic departments, NIL organizations, research groups, and technology providers.

## Five-Year Vision

Within five years, Portal Pulse should aim to become the trusted intelligence layer for college athletics: public enough for fans, powerful enough for media, structured enough for developers, and credible enough for professional team and department workflows.
