# Portal Pulse Network

## Mission

Portal Pulse Network is the collaboration and knowledge-sharing layer of Portal Pulse. It helps fans, analysts, media members, researchers, coaches, administrators, and future enterprise users organize intelligence, share evidence, publish structured reports, and build trusted expertise around college athletics movement.

Portal Pulse Network is not social media, a message board, or a discussion forum. It is a professional intelligence ecosystem.

## Network Philosophy

Every Network feature should support at least one goal:

- increase understanding
- improve collaboration
- surface quality intelligence
- encourage evidence-based discussion
- reward expertise over popularity

The Network should make high-quality intelligence easier to find, evaluate, save, and reuse across Portal Pulse.

## Professional Collaboration Principles

- Evidence comes before opinion.
- Confidence must be visible.
- Projections are labeled.
- Simulations are labeled.
- Verified placeholders are distinct from real verification.
- Reports should be structured, versioned, and evidence-linked.
- Popularity should never outrank trust.
- Future private collaboration belongs in protected enterprise workspaces.

## Product Areas

- **Network Dashboard:** today's intelligence, trending players/programs, expert analysis, evidence feed, confidence feed, recommended reads, popular watchlists, communities, and Knowledge Exchange.
- **User Profiles:** display name, role, sports, favorite programs, expertise tags, verification placeholders, saved reports, published reports, shared watchlists, reputation, contribution history, bookmarks, and AI preferences.
- **Intelligence Feed:** transfer updates, program news, reports, AI insights, watchlist changes, trending analysis, historical highlights, and community publications.
- **Reports:** structured player evaluations, program analysis, recruiting reports, transfer analysis, NIL trend reports, historical comparisons, conference reports, and market analysis.
- **Shared Watchlists:** athlete, program, conference, position, and transfer watchlists with sharing, pinning, sorting, filtering, collaboration placeholders, comments placeholders, and export placeholders.
- **Communities:** football, men's basketball, women's basketball, baseball, and softball communities powered by the Multi-Sport Foundation.
- **Portal Pulse Knowledge Exchange:** featured reports, editor's pick placeholders, featured analysts, trending insights, emerging storylines, historical perspective, and most trusted contributors.

## Reputation Engine

Reputation Score measures contribution quality, not popularity.

Factors include:

- published analysis
- evidence quality
- accuracy history placeholder
- community endorsements
- professional verification placeholder
- constructive participation
- report quality
- AI confidence alignment placeholder

Reputation should help users decide whose work to inspect, not whose claims to believe automatically. Claims still require evidence, confidence, source context, and Intelligence Core review.

## Moderation Philosophy

Moderation architecture prepares for:

- report review
- evidence verification
- community standards
- professional verification
- content flagging
- appeals
- moderation queue

Moderation is mock-only in this phase. No real moderation action, user enforcement, authentication, or private messaging exists.

## AI Integration

- Athena summarizes community intelligence.
- Atlas connects reports to roster intelligence.
- Mercury detects emerging market trends.
- Apollo adds historical context.
- Oracle creates clearly labeled forward-looking scenarios.
- Librarian indexes reports for semantic search.
- Cartographer maps relationships between athletes, coaches, programs, and reports.

Every AI output must identify verified placeholders, projections, confidence, and Explain This context. AI agents cannot publish facts or bypass the Intelligence Core.

## Future Enterprise Vision

Future enterprise Network capabilities may include:

- protected coach and GM collaboration
- private workspaces
- organization-level reports
- staff permissions
- evidence review workflows
- report approvals
- exportable briefings
- media partner intelligence hubs
- research workspaces
- API access to approved reports

These require authentication, authorization, audit logging, moderation, and enterprise data governance before production use.
