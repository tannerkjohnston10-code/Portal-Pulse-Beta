# Athena Specification

## Current Status

In Development — mock Chief Intelligence Officer page, briefing foundation, and agent network architecture exist. Real conversational AI and personalization are planned.

## Purpose

Athena is the Chief Intelligence Officer of Portal Pulse. Athena coordinates specialized agents, creates explainable briefings, answers questions from verified intelligence, and helps users understand what matters without publishing unsourced conclusions.

## Target Users

- Fans, media, and analysts needing briefings.
- Coaches and athletic departments needing decision support.
- Internal admin and review teams.
- Future API, mobile, and enterprise consumers.

## Primary Workflows

1. Open Athena to review daily briefing, latest verified intelligence, and agent network status.
2. Ask a placeholder question or choose a suggested prompt.
3. Review evidence-first answers, confidence, source counts, and approval status.
4. Follow links into Command Center, players, teams, predictions, TMX, Atlas, maps, or replay.
5. Receive personalized briefings in future versions.

## Core Features

- Athena status panel.
- Daily briefing with confidence and source summary.
- Latest verified mock intelligence.
- Agent network overview.
- Ask Athena placeholder.
- Suggested questions and evidence-first reminder.
- Intelligence Core approval status.

## Inputs

- Agent findings from Oracle, Sentinel, Librarian, Cartographer, Apollo, Mercury, Atlas, Vega, Hermes, and Archivist.
- Intelligence Core approved outputs, user preferences, watchlists, and historical context.

## Outputs

- Daily briefing, structured answers, recommended next actions, and briefing payloads.
- Future audio, mobile, API, and notification briefings.

## AI Responsibilities

Athena may summarize, coordinate, explain, and recommend. Athena must not invent facts, confirm NIL, present predictions as facts, or bypass the Intelligence Core. All findings are proposals until approved.

## Explainability

Athena responses must separate fact, claim, prediction, and analysis. Each answer should include why it matters, supporting evidence, confidence, contradictions, and what could change.
